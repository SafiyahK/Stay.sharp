Get Help, Stay Safe!

It's important to know that you're never alone if something online makes you feel uncomfortable or unsure.

Getting help is a smart and brave thing to do.



Why Get Help?

Stay Safe: Adults are here to help keep you safe both online and offline.

Understanding: Grown-ups can help you understand what's happening and guide you on the right actions to take.

Support: If you ever feel scared, upset, or confused, talking to a trusted adult can make a big difference.



Who Can You Talk To?

Parents and Guardians: Your mom, dad, or another adult in your family cares about you and wants to keep you safe.

Teachers: Teachers are there not just for lessons but also to help with any problems you might have.

School Counselors: They're experts in helping kids navigate through challenges and concerns.

Friends' Parents: If you're at a friend's house, their parents can help too.

Online Safety Officers: Some schools or organizations have special people to talk to about online safety.



When to Get Help?

Something Feels Wrong: Trust your instincts. If something doesn't feel right, it's okay to ask for help.

Unkind Behavior: If someone is being mean to you online or making you uncomfortable, don't hesitate to reach out.

Unwanted Messages: If you receive strange or inappropriate messages, share them with a trusted adult.



Remember:

No Secrets: Don't keep online troubles a secret. Share them with a grown-up you trust.

Quick Action: The sooner you ask for help, the quicker the problem can be solved.

You're Not Alone: There are many people ready to support you and ensure you have a positive online experience.
